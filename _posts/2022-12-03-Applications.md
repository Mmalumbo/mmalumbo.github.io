> Designed to function even when a device is not connected to the internet. At least, this is the definition that I have at this point for offline-first applications. The definition is short and easy to remember. So let's use my definition for this article.
{: .prompt-info }

Continuing on the topic, these types of applications prioritize the ability to perform tasks and access information locally on the device, rather than relying on a constant connection to a remote server.

Offline-first applications are useful in a variety of situations where an internet connection may not be available, such as when traveling on an airplane, in a remote location, or when experiencing network outages. They can also be beneficial in cases where internet connectivity is slow or unreliable, as they allow users to continue working without being hindered by delays caused by poor connection quality.

There are many different approaches to building offline-first applications, including the use of caching and local storage techniques, as well as synchronization mechanisms to ensure that data is updated and kept consistent across multiple devices.

Overall, offline-first applications provide users with a consistent and reliable experience, even when an internet connection is not available, making them an important consideration for developers looking to create apps that can function in a variety of different environments.
